## Overview

The RSS Sales Management Operating System (Sales MOS) is the management framework that governs how sales managers use the RSS methodology to manage their team's performance. It integrates three domains into a single operating rhythm:

1. **Process (Work)** -- the structured selling process defined by the RSS model (Positioning, Discovering, Building, Presenting, Resolving, Securing)
2. **Behaviours (Skills)** -- the selling skills and coaching competencies that RSS teaches
3. **Systems (Information)** -- the facts, metrics, and reporting that give managers visibility into sales activity and effectiveness

The IBC presentation (2003) states explicitly: **"Integration of all three areas is required to optimise and perpetuate full benefits."** The Sales MOS exists to ensure that RSS training does not remain a one-off classroom event but becomes embedded in daily management practice through coaching, structured reviews, and management by facts.

The RSS Trainer's Guide reinforces this: *"Both the salesperson and sales manager should recognise that the skills taught in RSS need to be monitored and enhanced on a continuing basis. Just as salespeople need to monitor and maintain their activity levels, they also need to monitor and maintain their effectiveness in front of the customer. Therefore, this workshop should be regarded as the beginning of a continuous process in which skills and call quality are enhanced on an ongoing basis through regularly scheduled coaching and counselling by the manager."*

### Relationship to the General Proudfoot MOS

The Proudfoot MOS framework (T3, 2021) defines a universal management cycle: **Forecast > Plan > Schedule > Implement > Report & Evaluate**. The Sales MOS applies this cycle to the sales function, translating the general MOS principles into sales-specific cadences and controls. The general MOS also provides concepts used in the Sales MOS context:

- **Short Interval Control (SIC)** -- comparing actual work against planned work at short intervals to identify variances before they become major problems
- **Active Management Behaviours (AMB)** -- defining what a manager does at each point during the day/week to maintain operational control
- **Leading Indicators** -- metrics that predict future outcomes rather than just reporting past results

## Managing by "Facts"

The IBC RSS presentation introduces the concept of **"Managing by the Facts"** as the core operating principle of the Sales MOS. This represents a shift:

- **From** reactive, opinion-based management ("I think the team is doing well")
- **To** proactive, fact-based management ("The data shows call volume is on target but conversion from Discovering to Building is lagging")

The "Facts" are generated by applying the MOS cycle to sales activities:

| MOS Stage | Sales Application |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **Forecast** | Sales forecast combining past volume trends with current pipeline data and market conditions to predict future revenue |
| **Plan** | Territory plans, account plans, and weekly/monthly call plans that organise how the forecasted workload will be managed |
| **Schedule** | Daily and weekly call schedules that allocate selling time to accounts and activities according to plan |
| **Implement** | Execution of the call schedule with short-interval controls to track progress against plan |
| **Report & Evaluate** | Performance reporting against planned targets, variance analysis, and corrective action planning |

## Pipeline Management

### The RSS Sales Model as Pipeline Framework

The RSS model provides the pipeline structure. Each sale progresses through stages defined by the customer's perception of needs and value, mapped on the Situational Matrix:

| Pipeline Stage | Customer State | Salesperson Focus |
|---------------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Positioning** | Low needs, low value perception | Establish credibility, purpose, and permission to proceed |
| **Discovering** | Emerging awareness of needs | Explore situation, problems, and needs through questioning |
| **Building** | Growing perception of need and urgency | Deepen awareness of impact, consequence, and urgency to act |
| **Presenting** | Needs defined, exploring solutions | Present options and recommend best solution tied to defined needs |
| **Resolving** | Concerns or objections surfacing | Address doubt, misinformation, or legitimate concerns |
| **Securing** | High needs, high value perception | Obtain commitment to order or to actions that advance the sale |

### Pipeline Review Process

The IBC RSS presentation defines a structured approach to pipeline reviews as part of the Sales Operating System. The RSS Interventions list includes:

- **Planning, Reporting, and dealing with variances** across:
  - Territory management
  - Schedule adherence
  - Meeting management (Preparation, Attendance, Debrief)
  - Developing action plans
  - Progress through the Sales Model
  - Training and skill development

## Activity Metrics

The RSS Trainer's Guide establishes two categories of sales performance indicators that the Sales MOS tracks:

### Quantitative Indicators (Activity Metrics)

These are **tangible measures of performance** that can be monitored and weighed against results:

- Number of customer contacts
- Number of demonstrations
- Number of surveys conducted
- Number of proposals delivered
- Call volume and frequency

The Trainer's Guide states: *"The quantitative indicators (numbers of contacts, demos, surveys, etc.) are important in that they are tangible measures of performance that can be monitored and weighed against results, providing the sales person with feedback and a means of adjusting activity levels to optimise results."*

### Qualitative Indicators (Skill Effectiveness)

These measure **call effectiveness** -- how well the salesperson uses RSS skills during customer interactions:

- Appropriate use of the Situational Matrix to read customer position
- Quality of questioning (open, closed, critical questions)
- Effectiveness of General Benefit Statements in positioning
- Ability to build customer perception of need and urgency
- Quality of presentation tied to discovered needs
- Skill in resolving concerns (distinguishing doubt, misinformation, and legitimate concerns)
- Effectiveness in securing commitments that advance the sale

The Trainer's Guide states: *"Of equal importance, the qualitative indicators have to do with call effectiveness. The time we spend with each customer or prospect is the most valuable time we have. Each salesperson should maximise the effectiveness of that time and constantly strive to improve in terms of selling skills."*

### The Activity-Effectiveness Balance

The Sales MOS requires managers to track **both** dimensions simultaneously. High activity with poor skill execution wastes customer time. Strong skills with insufficient activity leaves revenue on the table. The IBC presentation frames this through the integration model: Process (activity volume), Behaviours (skill quality), and Systems (the information that makes both visible).

## Coaching Cadence

### The Manager as Coach

The IBC presentation defines the desired behavioural shift for sales managers operating under the Sales MOS:

- **From** "reactive" management to **"proactive"** management by facts
- **From** a "policing" role to acting as **coaches, mentors, and trainers**

This aligns with the catalyst-coach model defined in the RSS Coaching Methodology: the manager creates an environment in which people want to succeed and provides them with the tools they need to succeed, rather than relying on close supervision and tight control.

### Coaching Structure

The IBC presentation identifies coaching as the connective tissue that links all elements of the Sales Operating System:

```
Training Sessions <-> Sales Operating System <-> Management by "FACTS"
                           |
                    COACHING - COACHING - COACHING
                           |
              Sales Effectiveness + Route Management
```

The RSS Trainer's Guide specifies that coaching is delivered through:

1. **Scheduled coaching calls** -- structured field observations where the manager accompanies the salesperson on customer calls
2. **Pre-call preparation** -- reviewing the account situation, setting call objectives, and planning the approach
3. **Post-call debrief** -- reviewing what happened against the plan, identifying what worked and what to improve
4. **Feedback delivery** -- using the Coaching Book's step-by-step framework for developmental feedback

The Coaching Book (referenced in the Trainer's Guide) contains:
- Step-by-step instructions on how to set the stage for coaching
- How to schedule and conduct coaching calls
- How to provide feedback
- Suggested actions on how to enhance sales skills
- Master copies of teaching schedules and critique forms

### Coaching Frequency

The Trainer's Guide positions coaching as **ongoing and continuous**: *"This workshop should be regarded as the beginning of a continuous process in which skills and call quality are enhanced on an ongoing basis through regularly scheduled coaching and counselling by the manager."*

## Performance Review Structure

### Dual-Dimension Review

Based on the RSS framework, performance reviews within the Sales MOS should assess both dimensions:

1. **Activity performance** -- Are activity levels sufficient to meet targets? Are territory and call plans being executed?
2. **Skill performance** -- Is the salesperson effectively using RSS skills? Where on the RSS model do they need development?

### Using the RSS Model for Skill Assessment

The Performance Objectives from the RSS Trainer's Guide provide the competency framework for skill assessment. Upon completion of RSS training, a salesperson should be able to:

- Assess a sale in process to determine the customer's perception of needs and benefits ("Hot Buttons")
- Determine the stage of the sale (Positioning, Discovering, Building, etc.) based on customer perception
- Develop a strategy or plan to advance the sale toward securing commitment
- Utilise appropriate skills in all phases of the sales process in response to the situation
- Develop a General Benefit Statement to establish purpose in positioning
- Utilise open and closed questions, and use acknowledgment or support to implement a discovering strategy
- Utilise critical questions that help the customer think about impact or consequence of need/value
- Present general options or alternatives, proposing or recommending a best solution
- Resolve customer concerns (doubt, misinformation, or legitimate concerns) by confirming and responding appropriately
- Secure customer commitment to an order or to actions that advance the sale

### Variance Management

Following the general MOS principle of **Report & Evaluate**, the Sales MOS requires managers to:

1. Compare actual performance against planned targets
2. Identify variances (both activity and skill dimensions)
3. Analyse root causes of variances
4. Define corrective actions
5. Follow up on corrective action execution

The IBC RSS Interventions list explicitly includes **"dealing with variances"** across territory, schedule, meetings, action plans, and progress through the Sales Model.

## Sales Manager Role

### The Shift in Management Approach

The Sales MOS fundamentally changes what a sales manager does. The IBC presentation defines this as two parallel shifts:

1. **From reactive to proactive** -- the manager uses facts and data (generated by the MOS) to anticipate issues and coach before problems materialise, rather than responding after targets are missed
2. **From policing to coaching** -- the manager's primary role becomes developing people through the catalyst-coach model rather than supervising through control and compliance

### Daily Management Under the Sales MOS

Drawing from both the RSS sources and the Proudfoot AMB (Active Management Behaviours) framework, the sales manager's role includes:

**Daily:**
- Review team activity data against schedule and plan
- Conduct short-interval check-ins on call outcomes and next actions
- Provide immediate coaching feedback on observed or reported customer interactions

**Weekly:**
- Review pipeline progress through the Sales Model stages
- Conduct scheduled coaching calls (field ride-alongs or call observation)
- Lead team meetings focused on variance review and best-practice sharing
- Update territory and call plans based on current pipeline state

**Periodic:**
- Conduct formal performance reviews assessing both activity and skill dimensions
- Review and update sales forecasts based on pipeline health
- Assess training needs and schedule skill development sessions
- Evaluate overall Sales MOS effectiveness and adjust cadences as needed

### Who Should Participate

The RSS Trainer's Guide is explicit: **"All salespeople and sales managers should participate in RSS."** Beyond this, it recommends that all levels of management in sales and marketing areas participate, because this ensures:

- A common vocabulary and understanding of the content and philosophy at all levels
- Increased commitment and endorsement from top management
- Increased conformance to the terms of the sales system and coaching elements throughout the organisation

## RSS Interventions Summary

The IBC presentation provides a complete list of RSS interventions that constitute the Sales Operating System in practice:

1. **Sales Operating System Installation** -- establishing the MOS framework and management rhythms
2. **Managing by the "Facts"** -- using the information generated by the system to drive decisions
3. **Planning, Reporting, and Dealing with Variances** covering:
   - Territory management
   - Schedule management
   - Meeting management (Preparation, Attendance, Debrief)
   - Action plan development
   - Progress tracking through the Sales Model
   - Training and skill development
4. **Coaching** -- the continuous reinforcement mechanism that connects all elements

The IBC presentation visualises this as a reinforcing system:

```
Training Sessions ←→ Sales Operating System ←→ Management by "FACTS" ←→ Sales Effectiveness
                                    ↕
                         Route Management
                                    ↕
              COACHING — COACHING — COACHING
                                    ↕
                        Outside Influencers
              (Maintenance, Production, Shipping)
```
